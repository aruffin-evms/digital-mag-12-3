New joint venture aligns future of IVF with field’s historic past

In 1987, Richard Scott, MD, was one of the first fellows to work with Drs. Howard and Georgeanna Jones, in vitro fertilization pioneers and namesakes of the EVMS Jones Institute for Reproductive Medicine. Now, more than 40 years later, Dr. Scott, Chief Executive Officer for IVI-RMA Global, is returning to the Jones Institute with an arsenal of tools to improve fertility success rates.

A new partnership between the Jones Institute and RMA Virginia, an internationally recognized leader in reproductive medicine and research, is bringing him back.

The joint venture pairs RMA’s unparalleled IVF success rates with the groundbreaking achievements of the Jones Institute — including the first successful IVF conception and birth in the United States.

RMA, through its medical and technological advancements in the field of reproductive medicine, has drastically reduced the risk to mother and child by routinely practicing single embryo transfer. Additionally, babies conceived using RMA’s method have birth weights equivalent to babies conceived naturally.

“The results speak for themselves,” says Alfred Abuhamad, MD, the Mason C. Andrews Chair in Obstetrics and Gynecology, Vice Dean for Clinical Affairs, and Professor and Chair of Obstetrics and Gynecology. “We are excited to have an opportunity to bring these impressive success rates to our patients and expand on the legacy we have established thanks to both Drs. Howard and Georgeanna Jones.”
